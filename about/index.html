About

My work sits at the intersection of art, technology, and teaching. I'm an artist and educator — a photographer by training and practice, a programmer by more recent study, and a teacher for more than twenty years.

I've been an Assistant Professor of Digital Photography at Montclair State University since 2004, where I've also directed the MFA in Studio Arts and, more recently, developed and taught courses in creative coding. My artistic practice explores digital and photographic processes; I hold a PhD from the University of the West of England on the digital recreation of nineteenth-century photographic printing methods.

In 2017, I completed a software development program at Fullstack Academy in New York. That same year I began working with Vidcode, an edtech startup whose premise was that coding and creativity belong together — that a student making a video filter or an animated portrait is doing real programming, and that a teacher doesn't need to be a computer scientist to guide that work.

Vidcode built a JavaScript-based platform for middle and high school students and designed it deliberately to be subject-agnostic: it could live in an art room or a history classroom, a coding elective or a science lab. It also had a strong equity dimension — the platform was meant to reach students who don't typically see themselves in computer science.

This portfolio documents the curriculum work I contributed to there: a three-course sequence that taught JavaScript through creative projects, and a standalone lesson built around Earth Day and environmental awareness.
